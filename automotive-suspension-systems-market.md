<p><p>The automotive suspension systems market comprises three primary types: passive, semi-active, and active suspension systems. Passive suspension systems utilize fixed components to absorb shocks, providing a stable ride. Semi-active systems adjust their response based on road conditions, enhancing comfort and handling. Active suspension systems dynamically alter their settings in real-time, optimizing performance and ride quality by responding to changing vehicle dynamics and road surfaces. Each type offers distinct advantages, catering to varying consumer preferences and vehicle performance requirements.</p></p>
