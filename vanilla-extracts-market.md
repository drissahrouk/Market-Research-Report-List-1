<p><p>The vanilla extract market is divided into two primary types: liquid and powder. Liquid vanilla extract, derived from vanilla beans steeped in alcohol, is widely used for baking and flavoring, offering a rich aroma and taste. Powdered vanilla extract, made from dehydrated vanilla beans, provides a convenient alternative for dry mixes and baked goods, maintaining flavor without added moisture. Both types cater to diverse culinary applications, appealing to both home bakers and professional chefs, each preferring their specific format for optimal flavor integration.</p></p>
