<p><p>The biological organic fertilizers market encompasses two primary types: microorganisms and organic residues. Microorganism-based fertilizers utilize beneficial bacteria and fungi to enhance soil health and fertility, promoting nutrient absorption and plant growth. Organic residues, on the other hand, consist of decomposed plant and animal materials, enriching soil with organic matter and essential nutrients. Both types improve soil structure, boost crop yields, and support sustainable agriculture by reducing reliance on synthetic fertilizers and fostering ecological balance.</p></p>
