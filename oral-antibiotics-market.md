<p><ul><li>Beta Lactam and Beta Lactamase Inhibitors</li><li>Quinolone</li><li>Macrolide</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The oral antibiotics market encompasses several key classes. Beta-lactams, which include penicillins and cephalosporins, are widely used for bacterial infections by disrupting cell wall synthesis. Beta-lactamase inhibitors augment beta-lactam efficacy against resistant bacteria. Quinolones are synthetic antibiotics effective against a broad spectrum of infections, targeting DNA replication. Macrolides, known for their broad antibacterial activity, inhibit protein synthesis. Together, these classes offer a diverse range of options to combat various bacterial infections effectively.</p></p>
