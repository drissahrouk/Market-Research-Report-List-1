<p><p>The liver biopsy market is categorized into three primary types: percutaneous, transjugular, and laparoscopic biopsies. Percutaneous liver biopsy involves a needle insertion through the skin to obtain a tissue sample, making it the most common method. Transjugular biopsy is performed by accessing the liver through a vein in the neck, ideal for patients with clotting disorders. Laparoscopic biopsy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure allowing direct visualization of the liver, often used for more complex cases. Each method is chosen based on patient needs and clinical circumstances.</p></p>
