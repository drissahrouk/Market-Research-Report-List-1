<p><p>The pediatric cranial remolding orthoses market consists of two main types: active helmets and passive helmets. Active helmets are designed with adjustable components that can be modified over time to promote optimal head shape correction. In contrast, passive helmets maintain a fixed shape and allow natural head growth while providing cushioning. Both types aim to address conditions such as plagiocephaly and brachycephaly in infants, offering therapeutic solutions tailored to individual cases to ensure healthy cranial development.</p></p>
