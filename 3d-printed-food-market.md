<p><p>The 3D printed food market encompasses several techniques. Extrusion-based printing involves using food pastes to create intricate shapes layer by layer. Selective laser sintering and binder jetting rely on using powdered ingredients, where lasers or binders fuse the materials to form solid structures. Inkjet printing disperses liquid food inks onto substrates, allowing for detailed designs and colors. Each method offers unique applications and advantages, catering to diverse culinary needs and enabling customization in food production.</p></p>
