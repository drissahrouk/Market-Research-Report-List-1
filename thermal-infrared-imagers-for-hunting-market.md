Uncooled thermal imagers use sensors that operate at ambient temperatures, making them lightweight and cost-effective, ideal for recreational hunters. On the other hand, cooled thermal imagers have sensors that operate at very low temperatures, providing higher sensitivity and improved image quality, suitable for professional use or specialized hunting scenarios. Each type caters to varying user needs based on performance and budget.</p></p>
